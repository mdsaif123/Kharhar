description

In our village, the local mosque is a cherished place of worship where all five daily prayers are held, along with the Friday Jumu'ah prayer. The people takes great care of the mosque, ensuring it remains well-maintained and welcoming. An Imam leads the prayers and provides spiritual guidance to the congregation.

Every month, a group (jammat) sets out from our mosque for religious activities and to spread the teachings of Islam. This mosque, beloved by the villagers, was built through the collective efforts of the entire community. It can accommodate at least 500 people at a time for prayer.

Inside the mosque, there is a designated area for ablution (wudu) where worshippers can perform their pre-prayer cleansing. On Fridays, due to the larger turnout, people also pray on the mosque's rooftop. The dedication of the villagers to both the upkeep and the spiritual activities of the mosque highlights its significance in our community.
